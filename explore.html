Magome–Tsumago Trail

馬籠・妻籠／中山道（サムライロード）

An 8 km section of the old Nakasendo highway connecting two beautifully preserved Edo-period post towns. A gentle, well-marked forest walk once travelled by samurai and merchants between Tokyo and Kyoto.

Naegi Castle Ruins

苗木城跡

A dramatic "sky castle" built atop giant boulders. The viewing platform offers a sweeping 360° panorama over Mt. Ena and the Kiso River, and it's a celebrated spot for early-morning seas of cloud.

Ryujin Falls (Kawakami)

川上・竜神の滝（青い滝）

A hidden cascade plunging into a vivid blue basin deep in the Kawakami valley. Named for the dragon god said to dwell here, it's a cool, quiet retreat surrounded by untouched forest.

Tsukechi Gorge & Fudo Falls

付知峡・不動の滝（ヒノキの森）

One of central Japan's most scenic gorges, with emerald pools, granite rock and the powerful Fudo Falls. The surrounding hinoki cypress forest is among the finest in the country—stunning in fresh green and autumn colour alike.

Mt. Fujimidai

富士見台高原（星空）

A grassy highland summit with views said to reach Mt. Fuji on the clearest days. After dark, the open ridgeline becomes one of the region's best places to take in a brilliant canopy of stars.

Nakatsugawa Town Stroll

中津川・街歩き

Wander the historic streets of Nakatsugawa-juku, a former Nakasendo post town. Old sake breweries, sweet shops and merchant houses line the way—use the illustrated map to explore at your own pace.
